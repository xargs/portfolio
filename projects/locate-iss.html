    Microfulfillment App

    Goal:

    • A hackathon organized by Dematic to build an android app for in-store fulfillment. The project was to design and develop features for the app.

    • Project timeline: 4 days.

    • Team size: 3 .

    Execution:

    • My tasks:

      • Volunteered to build APIs to integrate with back-end data providing and persisting APIs via REST service with JSON payload.

      • Provided factory methods to be used within front-end framework, for eg: displaying items to be picked for an order, persisting a pick after the worker confirms it on the app etc.

      • Provided the capability to scan via camera for a given SKU (Stock Keeping Unit) making it easy for in-store worker to pick products from aisles.

      • Built a test suite to test all points of data contracts.

    • Rest of the team's tasks:

      • One team member volunteered to setup android app and build navigational screens, while the other team member used the APIs I created to plug in within workflows of the app.

     

     

    Things I learned:

    • First time experience building an android app. Never realized until then that's its easy, intuitive and fun to build phone apps.

    • Extensive use of RESTful APIs proved to make pulling data seamless and easily pluggable into front end framework.

    • Our hackathon win proved that team work leads to successful outcomes. Learning something new within a short time span is a challenge, but can be accomplished with right mind set. Kudos to the team!
